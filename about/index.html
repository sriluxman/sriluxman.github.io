About

Close to the machine. Thinking in platforms.

I lead product for next-generation industrial automation at ABB — and I still build things with my own hands. This page is the story so far, told from now.

I care about the point where technology becomes useful to real people. In industrial automation, that point is rarely clean. Machines live for decades, customer environments differ, and engineering workflows carry history for good reasons. I have spent my career moving toward that messy point from both directions — from the machine floor up, and from product strategy down.

Now — realizing next-generation industrial software, one service at a time

2024 – present · Technical Product Manager · Austria

I was instrumental in helping develop the vision for the next generation of industrial software together with product leadership — and now I work on making it real. As Technical Product Manager I lead the way from abstract vision to tangible, release-ready product, serving as technical lead for a talented, geographically distributed team. The work is equal parts product judgment, architecture conversations, and keeping distributed engineering honest about what "done" means.

A growing part of my energy goes into agentic engineering: understanding what it takes for AI to participate in industrial engineering without breaking its trust model. I approach that the way I approach everything here — grounded in workflows, skeptical of demos, serious about what a platform has to promise before anyone relies on it.

How I operate has stayed constant across roles: I move toward complex, future-facing problems and help turn them into structured strategies, reusable platforms, and product-shaped systems. My natural first move is to build — when a vision is still abstract, I prototype a reference architecture and a mockup myself, so everyone can see a glimmer of the end state instead of arguing about slides. I dream big, experiment, fail fast, and increment; and I hold just as firmly that agility does not mean chaos. Requirements engineering and architecture are the harnesses that keep the creative beast pulling in one direction.

I believe platforms are trust systems, that meaningful harmonization beats forced standardization, and that no architecture is honest until it has survived commissioning.

Where this is heading

I am inspired by the next scale of responsibility: leading product management, or product and engineering direction for industrial software — the portfolios, the teams, and the connective tissue between strategy and engineering reality. I am not there yet, and I am deliberate about preparing for it: taking on the accountability in reach, studying how good product organizations work, and building the judgment that scale demands. Industrial automation is rebuilding itself around software-defined systems and AI, and I want to carry real responsibility in that rebuild.

That ambition grows from a good place. I love the work and the people around me today, and the strongest next chapters are usually built on chapters that are going well. Everything on this site — the writing, the experiments, the running log — is the working record of that preparation.

The foundation

Everything above stands on three earlier chapters. In reverse order:

Platform engineering and DevOps strategy

2021 – 2024 · Product Owner, ABB

I developed DevOps strategy for R&D product and service teams and drove platform engineering with experts across the organization: reducing duplicated effort, reusing what already worked, filling capability gaps, and creating pull for shift-left practices. This work contributed to the start of a central DevOps strategy unit that later grew significantly. It taught me organizational influence — how to change the way many teams work without having authority over any of them.

Test and release management

2015 – 2021 · Project Leader, ABB

I owned test and release strategy for mechatronic software products: durable, platform-agnostic automation across static and dynamic testing, ISTQB-informed test-pyramid thinking, and the standing bridge between engineering, product, and customer-facing teams. It taught me that quality is a product feature, and that trust is built one release at a time.

Application engineering

2009 – 2015 · International Application Engineer, B&R

I started close to machines and customers, working with OEMs and system integrators on complex machine applications — especially advanced motion — from requirements through commissioning and series readiness, and teaching through B&R's Automation Academy. It gave me the thing no later role can: a physical respect for what industrial software has to survive outside the slide deck.

The lab

Away from the roadmap I keep a small lab of running experiments: agentic engineering environments, a remote 3D-printing workflow, an agent that composes music for stepper motors, requirements tooling built on MCP, and an LLM-maintained knowledge wiki pattern I am testing against real engineering lifecycles.

The lab is not a hobby footnote. It is how I keep product judgment calibrated against what is actually possible — and it is why my interest in cloud-native behavior, platform engineering, and AI is grounded in working systems, not labels.
