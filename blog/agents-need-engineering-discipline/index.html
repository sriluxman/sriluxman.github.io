Agents need engineering discipline

An agent is not magic labor. It is an unreliable engineering participant with tools.

March 21, 2026

Agentic engineering will expose your engineering culture.

An agent is not a magic worker. It is an unreliable coworker with APIs.

That sounds negative. It is actually useful. The moment an agent starts doing real work, vague assumptions become visible. Permissions become product decisions. Tool descriptions become contracts. Logs become the difference between learning and guessing.

This is especially important in industrial systems. A failed blog draft is cheap. A bad change in an engineering workflow can cost a commissioning day, a customer relationship, or machine availability.

The answer is not to keep agents away from meaningful work forever. The answer is to design participation: bounded tools, typed outputs, visible decisions, reversible changes, and clear ownership.

Autonomy without observability is just hidden work.

Agentic engineering will not remove the need for discipline. It will make the lack of discipline visible.
