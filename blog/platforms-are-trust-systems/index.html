Essay

Platforms are trust systems

Self-service is not a portal feature. It is a promise that the platform will behave.

May 11, 2026

Most automation platforms do not have a technology problem. They have a trust problem.

Teams say they want self-service. Users still open a ticket because the documented path fails in the interesting cases.

Platform engineering is often presented as internal tooling. That misses the hard part. A platform is a trust system.

Can users predict what will happen? Can they recover when it does not? Can the platform explain its state without requiring access to the people who built it?

This is harder in industrial automation. The users are not one homogeneous development team. They include machine builders, application engineers, service technicians, and product teams with different timelines and risks.

A portal can hide complexity. It cannot create trust by itself.

The platform becomes useful when the paved road is not only easy on the happy path, but honest when reality becomes messy.
