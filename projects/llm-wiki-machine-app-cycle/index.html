Project / building

LLM wiki for machine application cycles

A maintained knowledge system for understanding how machine applications evolve across engineering, commissioning, and service.

Machine application knowledge is scattered across requirements, code, commissioning notes, issue trackers, and the memories of experienced people. When one of those people leaves, or a machine comes back for service five years later, the scattering becomes a real cost.

This project applies the LLM-maintained wiki pattern to the lifecycle of a machine application: an agent curates raw material — documents, notes, captures — into a maintained, linked knowledge base with an explicit record of what changed, what conflicts, and what is still uncertain.

The pattern

The core discipline is separation: raw sources stay immutable, the curated wiki carries the current understanding, and every claim in the wiki traces back to a source. Contradictions are recorded instead of silently smoothed over — which turns out to be the feature that matters most, because engineering knowledge is full of claims that were true at commissioning and false by the third service visit.

I run this pattern personally to test it, and the machine-application version asks whether it survives contact with an industrial lifecycle: engineering hand-offs, commissioning discoveries that never make it back into the documentation, and service knowledge that lives in one technician’s head.

Why it matters

Long-lived machines are accumulated decisions. A knowledge system that keeps those decisions legible across roles and years is not a documentation nicety — it is the difference between servicing a machine and re-reverse-engineering it. If agents are going to help with industrial engineering at all, this is the substrate they will need.
