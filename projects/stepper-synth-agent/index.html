Project / exploring

Stepper motor synth agent

An agent that composes music for the mechanical voice of stepper motors.

Stepper motors already make music. Usually it is accidental — the whine of a print head, the chorus of an axis move. Anyone who has spent years around motion control knows the sound.

This project makes it deliberate: an agent that composes for the constraints of motors, motion, resonance, and timing. It sits somewhere between automation, generative music, and a machine learning to perform.

Why compose for motors

Because constraints are the interesting part. A stepper motor is a terrible instrument: limited pitch range, timbre coupled to load, and timing dictated by physics rather than taste. Composing for it means the agent cannot generate abstract music and hope — it has to reason about what this specific hardware can physically voice, at what speed, without stalling.

That makes it a compact study of a serious question: how do you give an agent creative freedom inside hard physical constraints? The answer looks a lot like good motion engineering — respect the limits, plan the trajectory, and treat every violation as information.

Where it stands

An exploration, and happily so: motor control experiments, first composed sequences, and a growing feel for which musical ideas survive translation into torque and steps. It is the least useful project in the lab and the one that best explains why I find machines worth listening to.
